About the visualizations

Scatterplot

The scatter plot is a uniform random sample of all data-points in the given timeframe with the given sample-size.
Zooming is possible via click-hold selection. A double click resets the time axis and custom sample-size.

Lineplot

The lineplot shows a connection of all data points by a line. The color gradient highlights the variations in the size of the y-variable.
Zooming is possible via click-hold selection. A double click resets the time axis.

Barchart

The bar chart shows the cumulative sum of activity (steps or calories) within the given time interval. By clicking on bar, the activity within the interval of that bar is plotted more precisely. Additionally, zooming is possible via click-hold selection. A double click resets the time axis to the whole data set within the chosen precision.

Boxplot

The box plot is a data distribution visualization of all data-points in the given timeframe.

Bubblechart

The Bubblechart plots circular activity. Every bubble represents an hour within a weekday (e.g. 0:00-1:00 on Monday). The mean activity for every timepoint was either computed by the mean within an hour (temperature, heartrate) or by the sum within an hour (steps, calories).

Chordgraph

The chordraph represents the magnitudes of correlation (computed by using the amount of the pearson correlation coefficient between all data points) between each two variables.
The variables are represented as nodes on the outer part of the circular layout. Arcs connect the nodes and depict the magnitude of the correlation. By hovering over a variable name the corresponding arcs are highlighted in the graph. The arcs are colored by the color specified for the respective variable.

Correlation Analysis of daily data

This visualization contains a correlation analysis of the data aggregated per day. In this analysis, the sum of calories and steps is determined, and the mean value is determined for temperature and heart rate. The correlation measure (Pearson correlation coefficient) is automatically displayed. Regression equations can be inserted through the data points and linear, quadratic and polynomial (order=3) regression equations can be displayed with the corresponding R2-value.
